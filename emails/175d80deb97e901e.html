Hey, are you looking for something new to listen to on your next long run or ride? Us too! With races being sidelined, we realized how much we miss talking about them - the long hours training, the pre-race jitters, and the glory...oh, that sweet, sweet glory.

But wait! We don’t need races to talk about racing - we can have those conversations right now...so we decided to LAUNCH A PODCAST!

Each show will be a 60 to 90 minute interview with some of the millions of athletes who have been crossing finish lines around the world - telling the stories behind the steps, the pedals, and the swim strokes. The blood sweat and tears, the successes and failures of finishing, balancing work and life while training and racing. We're also going to be talking with event organizers and other folks who make the products and services that we, as endurance athletes, love and rely on to accomplish our goals.

We will be releasing new shows every Tuesday and Thursday with the occasional bonus show on Saturday morning and we already have our first 6 episodes available today in your favorite podcast apps.

Episode 1: Todd Straka
@boulderrunner

Listen in to Todd and Troy discuss everything from what it means to be a runner (AND event organizer) in the midst of a pandemic to being fast after 50.
 

 
Episode 2: Frankie Ruiz
Miami Marathon

Dig into Frankie's passion for all levels of running and how he's been bringing a healthier lifestyle to the city of Miami for 15+ years. #dontstop 
 

 
Episode 3: Mike Milici
ChronoTrack

Mike gives an inside look into the technology backing the race industry, how it's been affected by COVID, and his journey through setting a big race goal.
 

 
Episode 4: Ted Kennedy
AchieveRacing

Join our conversation with Ted about his role in the early days of Ironman and how he's since brought hundreds of executives together to cross epic finish lines around the world.

 
Episode 5: The Saches
An Intentional Marriage + Goals

Chris and Lindsey Sachs drop by to talk about their intentional marriage, goal setting as a family and how to balance it all when BOTH are 100-mile racing endurance athletes.
 

 
Episode 6: Nick Symmonds
2x Olympian & RunGum

Nick walks us through some of his racing memories, his thoughts on the supression of athlete rights and the need to pay amateur athletes, and his journey with RunGum.
