A Glass 5% Full

Yesterday an Athlinks supported article ran in the Wall Street Journal documenting the effects of the Coronavirus on the endurance racing industry. Through our analysis of the Athlinks results database, we found that event cancellations have led to a 95% drop (94.77% to be exact) in timed athletes in the U.S. since March 15th. While this number does not include the bump from Virtual Races that we have seen since March (around 15-20%) , it provides a valuable insight into the health of our industry.

There are two ways to look at these stark statistics; either the glass is 95% empty or the glass is 5% full. Between January 1 and March 15, finisher counts in the U.S. were actually up 3% over 2019 - the first year-over-year upward trend since 2012. And, during COVID, the run and bike specialty industries have experienced unprecedented growth as a surging number of people, hungry for the mental and physical benefits that an active lifestyle provides, flock to their local run and bike shops. There has been no decline in demand - which is part of why I see the glass as 5% full, rather than 95% empty.

And in the meantime, tens of thousands of event operators have been effectively shut down. Preparing for the inevitable return to racing, these professionals are regrouping, re-mapping race routes to factor in safety, social distancing and cost structures, race formats, etc.

The art of putting on races is quite literally the alchemy of ideas. A race organizer brings their expertise and imagination to create experiences enjoyed by their participants. Other than lead time for permits and marketing, the “where”, “when” and “how” events can occur is only limited by one’s imagination. The best way to ensure that your favorite events come back in the future is to offer your encouragement and support to those race organizers. Let them know that you will be there when they come back, ready to toe that start line … and bring a friend. Email them, text them, sign up for a virtual race they are organizing, reach out on social media - just take a few minutes to let your enthusiasm be felt.

Happy Racing Everybody - Virtual or otherwise - Until next time.
