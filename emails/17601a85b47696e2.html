It’s Thanksgiving here in the U.S., historically the largest racing day of the entire year. We’ve had year after year of a record breaking number of finishers, and running a local 5k has become an established tradition for individuals and families alike.

2020 is bringing plenty of change, including how you’re likely spending your Thanksgiving morning. We continue to be thankful for the local race organizers who persevere, finding new and innovative ways for us to race. If you happen to have an in-person turkey trot in your area, we’d encourage you to support them and race it! If not, we urge you to keep tradition alive — grab a family member, go for a run, spread some cheer and wave at the other runners you pass on their pre-turkey 5k. Either way, we’ll all be bound by that one common thing, our love for the sport and we’re thankful for that.

From all of us at Athlinks, thank you.

Happy Racing,
Troy Busot
