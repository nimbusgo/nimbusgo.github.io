5 Ways To Support Yourself This Holiday Season

Feeling overwhelmed by parenthood and/or the impending holiday season and coronavirus? If you're like me, you could definitely use some support right now. The holidays can bring on a sense of chaos and lack of ease-and it's ok to have these feelings.

You can be rest assured, it only takes one small change to create more ease in our lives and the lives of those around us. Start small (pick one option from below) and build from there to bring a sense of ease and grounding to the holiday season.
  1. Move your body in a way that feels good for you. Pick a favorite song and dance by yourself or with your child.
  2. Recharge by taking a guilt- and shame-free 30-minute break from your child(ren) to do something you love. Take your legs up the wall, read a magazine, whatever brings you joy will help with grounding.
  3. Take a 60 second child's pose. Relax and let the ground hold you.
  4. Do one thing for YOU before doing dishes or laundry while your child takes a nap. The dishes can wait (and honestly, do they ever end?).
  5. Create a Vision Board for 2021. Join Nicole on January 9th for a free workshop!

The C-Section Project 
by Katrina Nevin

"In the first few days after my baby was born by emergency C-section I was amazed to discover how completely unprepared I was for the experience. I desperately wanted to talk with someone who could validate and explain the intense physical pain and emotional upheaval I was experiencing. 

The hospital gave out a generic printed sheet of information about post-surgery rest, diet and incision care but I wanted something more comprehensive. I wanted practical advice and compassionate guidance telling me what to expect and what to do to make things easier.
That's why I created the C-section project. This is a dedicated space for specific support and advice drawn directly from the experiential wisdom of many women. This is an encyclopedia and user manual in one, and a gathering place for stories, hard-earned knowledge and emotional healing. It is a comprehensive and intimate resource for women who are struggling to get up to speed in the aftermath of what is often an unexpected or unwanted major abdominal surgery: being unzipped." 

So far there are 18 people (and myself) who have shared their stories/advice in the collection. I have heard from many of them that the process of writing their own birth story was healing and gave them a feeling of closure, even many years after their cesarean birth took place. If you or anyone you know wants to share their story on the website they can email me at thecsectionproject@gmail.com.

Visit the site, connect with others and learn more at csectionproject.com.
