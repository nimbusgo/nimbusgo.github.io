Dear Prema Yoga Family-


We have somehow found ourselves well into November, gearing up for another Holiday Season. At our home studios in Brooklyn, we're headed into the darkest and coldest time of the year, a time typically of hibernation and introspection as we sync into the rhythms of nature.

Our yoga practice this month centers on Accountability. A cynical view on this time of year only sees hyper-consumerism and superficiality, but completely misses the ways we demonstrate care and love for each other through celebrations, rituals, and gift-giving. In this era of COVID-19, our Holiday celebrations and traditions are certainly not going to be the same as they have been in the past. We are being challenged and asked to adapt in ways we've never had to before.

There is one particular tradition we at Prema Home are opting out of: Black Friday. This "tradition" is a naked product of consumerism, relies on exploiting resources and labor, and whips up a false frenzy around Holiday shopping.

We want to extend our thanks to our Prema family beyond Black Friday and to make the season less stressful.

Accountability and gratitude both take practice, facing hard truths, and persistence. And yet accountability is not joyless, and gratitude is definitely not shallow. We hope that we can help you bring ethically made goods into your homes and into the lives of your loved ones this Holiday Season.

HEALTHIER HIPS
WITH GRETCHEN WILLIAMS

During the first 10 years of the century, the total number of hip replacements more than doubled among adults between 45-54 years. That astounding trend will likely continue without a hard look into how we work with our hips within our own asana practice, regardless of age.

More often than not, a yoga class features some sort of “hip openers” - shapes and poses which ask our hip joints to become more flexible and mobile, increasing our range of motion. Due to the lack of nerves within joint tissues, we may not even realize we’re going too deep, and over time we wear and tear the precious joint cartilage, which doesn’t grow back. The result is p painful bone spurs, or hip and knee replacements.

As we work to become more flexible, we must cultivate strength and stability in and around the joints, keeping us safe and supported in all of our activities.

In this workshop, we’ll explore the anatomy of the hip system - the five bones (ischium, ilim, pubis, sacrum and two femur bones) and the five major joints that connect them (two hip joints, two sides of the sacroiliac (SI) joint, and the pubic symphysis). Understanding how they’re inextricably connected with each other, and with the knee, will allow you to move in a more informed, safe way both on and off the mat.

We’ll prepare joint tissues and ligaments utilizing isometric drills, discuss common yoga cues which potentially compromise your hip and knee joints, and teach the nervous system how to safely explore functional range of motion.

While we can’t adjust our genetics and joint shape, we can become more aware of how our unique anatomy and movement patterns affect the health of our hips and knees.
